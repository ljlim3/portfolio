Student Dashboard & Other Features

Student Dashboard

User Problem

Students in different break-out rooms should know what students in other break-out rooms are doing.

Ideation

The student dashboard

I came up with different use cases of the dashboard, potential side effects, goals, and ideas that takes these into account.

Use Cases

  • Are other classmates participating?

  • How are others participating (understand others' participation)

  • What do I need to do? When is the deadline for tasks?

  • Progress: What tasks have I completed? What are tasks yet to be done? Where are others?

  • Show status of each breakout group: "Things are going well", "We're confused", "We're done", etc.

Goals, Positive Effects & Potential Side Effects

Goals | Positive Effects

  • Motivate participation
  • Provide directions (e.g. how to engage in activity)
  • Foster group identification and teamwork


Potential Side Effects

  • Social influence - if students see others NOT participating, they also may be encouraged to NOT participate or do their homework.
Ideas

Leaderboard & Badges

Rank team based on the number of participations. Ranking will be updated real time. First place group or individual will get a badge.

  • First to contribute within group

  • First to contribute in class

  • First group to contribute

  • First to earn a badge

Redesigning Existing Features

I was requested to quickly redesign some of the existing features to make them look more middle-school-students-friendly. I started by sketching one of the features, namely the How Can I Earn a Badge dialog, and then prototyped all the other features based on and in consistent style with the sketch.
