UbiCoS

Teacher Dashboard

What's UbiCoS?

Ubiquitous Collaboration Support

is an intelligent learning web application that provides three different math activities for middle school students, where the three activities are:

  • Discussion forum (post responses to other students’ questions)
  • Posting answers on Khan academy Q&A forum
  • Explaining mathematical concepts to a teachable agent

Students earn a badge for each meaningful contribution or help-giving interaction.

About Teacher Dashboard

Through the
teacher dashboard

teachers would like to understand student participation.

My Role

  • Design the After Class teacher dashboard from scratch using pencil & paper and InVision sketch tool

  • Present the designs in lab and team meetings for feedback

  • Conduct usability testing with a middle school teacher who will be the main user

  • Create high fidelity prototype using Figma

  • Implement using HTML and CSS

User Problem

Teacher wants a dashboard that shows summaries of student activities in an easily understandable manner, without having to perform many clicks in order to see an information.


Design Challenge

Showing the necessary information in a snapshot in an immediately clear and comprehensible manner (i.e. clearly convey who did not participate in what activities). Eliminating the need to dig in deep in order to get to an information.

Specifically, the teacher would like to view:

  • For each activity, who participated and who didn’t
  • For each student, what activities they participated and not participated in
  • How many badges each student earned
  • For what contributions the badges were earned
Design 1 – Iteration 1

Contemplating on the requirements, I have identified the problem to be organization of two-dimensional data—one dimension being students and the other activities. Accordingly, I deemed a table to be a good structure to visually present the data in a snapshot.

For students in rows and activities in columns, I indicated whether a student participated or not in an activity using filled and hollow discs, respectively. Additionally, I included a column that shows the total activities each student either participated or not participated in, based on the radio button selection (Participated vs Not Participated) above the table. By default, this column is sorted in descending order of the total to draw attention to students who have higher number of either category (participated or not participated). Clicking on a student’s name brings user to the details page that shows how students participated.

Details:
The Dashboard information is organized on a weekly basis. So the Week dropdown provides all the past and current weeks as options users can choose from.

Design 1 – Iteration 2

As an alternative design, I organized activity into a tab that teachers can click into to see just the names of the students who, based on the radio button selection (Participated vs Not Participated), participated or not participated in the selected activity. By partitioning the activities, I tried to reduce crowdedness and simplify the interface.

Feedback:
I had the privilege to directly talk to the teacher who will be using the dashboard. For the above two designs, the teacher liked how the first one enabled her to see the participation status of her students all on one page; she liked the simplicity of the second design but thought it offered less-friendly user experience because she would have to click each tab to see if the same student did not participate in the other activities.

Design 2 – Iteration 1

What changed:
I broke down the activities by platforms (Discussion Forum, Khan Academy, and Teachable Agent). I kept the tabs but this time, I added two columns: one that shows other activities with the same participation status in the form of tags and one that shows the badges earned for the selected activity. All badges student earned can be seen on a new page if user clicks “more”.

Feedback:
I had a meeting with my team and they generally agreed that it wasn't immediately clear what the second column was indicating.

Design 3 – Iteration 1

What changed:
I removed the tabs and instead, displayed all the activities for the specified platform. The participation status is color-coded. There’s also a filter option, namely Not Participated Students, that shows only the activities in which a student did not participate.

Feedback:
I met up with the middle school teacher and her feedback was that it would be helpful to have an option to see the participation status for all the activities.

Final Design

What changed:
I added an ALL tab that shows all the activities across all three platforms. I removed the filter option because the teacher thought it wasn’t necessary, as being able to view all the activities provided useful context.

The teacher was happy with the design. The final design provides tabs for each learning platform (Discussion Forum, Khan Academy, Teachable Robot) as well as the “All” tab, where user can see all the activities across all platforms. The participation status is color-coded (i.e. green for participated, red for not-participated, and gray for activities not yet started).
